Organic Audience Explorer

Who we actually reach organically across our main social platforms — normalized so channels are comparable. Built to answer which channel is best for reaching which audience, not to compare raw volume. Sample windows differ slightly by platform.
Read me first. Figures are shares of each platform's audience, not absolute reach. Facebook shows followers (not reached audience) and its age split is estimated from the chart. YouTube reports Shorts and Videos as distinct audiences — they behave very differently, so they're kept separate. YouTube & TikTok geo percentages are share of total views (long global tail), while X / Instagram / Facebook geo is share of audience — compare geo within a platform, not across.

Female reach index

What it means

  • Instagram & Facebook are our female-skewing channels (~64% women).
  • TikTok tilts female (55%) but is the most balanced of the high-reach channels.
  • X is our most male channel (60% men) — and YouTube Shorts (63% men) is more male than Videos (51%).
  • If a campaign needs to reach men, X and YouTube Shorts are the efficient picks; for women, IG / FB / TikTok.

Device / surface mix

Only X and YouTube expose device data. The headline: YouTube Videos are watched on the TV (45%) — a lean-back, living-room surface — while everything else is phone-first.

Surface Phone-first

X is ~92% mobile (iOS 56% / Android 36%). TikTok, Instagram and YouTube Shorts are inherently phone-native. Creative must work sound-on, vertical, thumb-stopping in the first second.

Surface Living-room

YouTube Videos are 45% TV + 6% tablet — over half on a large screen. This is the one place long-form, higher-production trailers pay off. Shorts stay 65% mobile.

Surface iOS lean

Where reported, iOS over-indexes vs Android (X 56/36). Worth checking that landing pages / deep-links are iOS-optimized.

When each audience is active

Best posting windows as reported per platform. Times are local (GMT+2 / Spain).

Scheduling takeaways

  • Instagram peaks Sun 18-23h and Mon 21-23h — evening, front-of-week. Prime slot for weekend hype and Monday launches.
  • TikTok activity spikes very late (00-01h) — a night-owl audience; late-evening drops can catch the peak.
  • X peaks midweek (Wednesday) mornings/midday — align real-time / news-style posts here.
  • YouTube viewing builds through afternoon-evening across the whole week — less time-sensitive, more evergreen.
  • Combined: a title moment can roll X midday → YouTube afternoon → IG evening → TikTok late-night to ride each audience's peak in sequence.
