🏅 Golden rules of publishing
01
Choose format by objective, not by default
An Instagram launch Photo can outperform any Reel in raw reach (3.8M vs 705K max this week). Choose by goal: Photo/Carousel for impressions, Reel for ER.
02
Frequency is infrastructure
Facebook: dropping from 31 to 10 posts cut reach by 83%. Posting frequency is not optional — it's the foundation on which any result is built. Set a non-negotiable weekly minimum per channel.
03
Keep daily reactivity slots open
Real-time reactive content (matches, virals, trends) has an organic reach multiplier that can't be planned in advance. Keep at least 1 empty slot per day in the calendar to improvise.
04
Activate fandoms 2–3 weeks before release
Heartstopper, Bridgerton, Attack on Titan generate 2–4x ER above average without any boost. Identify fandom titles before launch and start community activation well in advance.
05
Local content outperforms global in ER
Local productions consistently deliver above-average ER. Prioritise local content editorially and localise copy for international titles with market-specific cultural references.
06
TikTok needs native content, not reposts
The algorithm detects and penalises duplicated content across platforms. Native TikTok videos generate 2–3x more ER. Creating at least 50% of the TikTok calendar natively is essential.
